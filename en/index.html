RANDOM COLORS ONLINE

Roll color dice online

Color Dice puts a random color picker into a familiar dice roll. Choose one to six dice. Each die lands on red, orange, yellow, green, blue, or purple. The result can decide a game move, classroom task, art palette, or any choice with up to six options.

The roller runs in your browser on desktop and mobile. You do not need to install anything or create an account. One die handles a single choice, while several dice make a sequence for a group activity or a set of colors for a project.

YOUR FIRST ROLL

How to use the color dice roller

01

Choose your dice

Select one to six color dice. Use one die for one decision, or several dice for a sequence, team assignment, or small palette.

02

Roll for colors

Press "Roll Again" to reveal every selected die at once. Mega Roll reveals the dice in order, while Scratch Card lets you uncover each result yourself.

03

Follow your rules

Read the dice from left to right and use the meanings you set before the roll. Press the button again when the next turn needs a new result.

CHOOSE THE NUMBER OF DICE

How many color dice should you roll?

Pick the number of dice before you roll. A single color works for one decision. Use several dice when order, teams, or a palette matters.

1

One die for one choice

Use one color die for a single action, player, topic, or category. Assign one option to each color before the roll, then follow the option that appears.

2-3

Two or three for a sequence

Combine the results into a short prompt. The first die can choose a subject, the second an action, and the third a difficulty level or time limit.

4-6

Four to six for groups

Use a larger set to assign colors to players, fill spaces on a board, or make a random palette. Give each person one die or read the results in order.

A color can appear more than once in a roll because the dice are independent. Decide what repeats mean before anyone rolls. You can do an action twice, give that color more weight, or reroll only the duplicate.

SET THE COLOR RULES

Ideas for color dice meanings

The colors do not have fixed meanings. Attach any six answers or actions that suit your game. This set works as a starting point:

Red

Stop, skip a turn, or take the harder task.

Orange

Switch direction, choose again, or use another method.

Yellow

Let the player choose, pause, or make up a new move.

Green

Continue, score a success, or complete an easy task.

Blue

Take another turn, answer a question, or choose a quiet action.

Purple

Add a bonus, raise the difficulty, or extend the sequence.

Write down the color key before anyone rolls. That keeps the rule the same for every player and every round.

THREE WAYS TO REVEAL

Regular roll, Mega Roll, and Scratch Card

🎲

Regular roll

Reveal all selected color dice together. Use this mode for repeated turns, classroom rotations, and quick decisions.

⚡

Mega Roll

The dice arrive and reveal their colors one by one. It works well for a final round.

🪙

Scratch Card

Each color starts under a silver cover. Scratch it with a mouse or finger. Keyboard users can focus a card and press Enter or Space.

IDEAS FOR EACH ROLL

Ways to use a random color dice roller

Classroom activities. Match colors with reading, speaking, drawing, movement, review, or a question. One roll picks the next task without putting the choice on a student.

Art prompts. Roll several dice for a drawing, collage, character, bead pattern, or pixel-art palette. A repeated color can take up more space in the finished work.

Board and party games. Let the colors stand for teams, resources, spaces, actions, or question categories. The online roller can replace a missing physical die and gives everyone the same visible result.

Movement breaks. Assign an exercise to each color and roll between work sessions. Red could mean jumping jacks, green stretching, and blue a short rest. Set repetitions separately.

Quick choices. Map six acceptable options to the colors. Agree on the mapping first, then use the color that appears.

Story and writing games. Use one die for a character's mood, another for a location, and a third for an event. A small set of color-coded lists can produce many prompts.

HOW THE COLORS ARE CHOSEN

How the random color roll works

For each roll, the browser uses its built-in cryptographic random number generator to choose a color for every die. All six colors have the same chance, and one die does not affect the next.

Independent rolls sometimes repeat a color. The last result and roll count stay in local browser storage on your device. The game does not require an account or send your roll history to a server.

COMMON QUESTIONS

Color dice roller FAQ

Answers about the dice, modes, colors, and random results.

What is a color dice roller?

A color dice roller is a random color generator presented as a set of virtual dice. Each die lands on one of six colors: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, or purple.

How do I roll color dice online?

Choose between one and six dice, then press "Roll Again." The random colors appear after a short animation.

Is the color dice roller free?

Yes. The regular roll, Mega Roll, and Scratch Card modes are free to use without an account.

What colors can I roll?

The six possible results are red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple.

Can I roll more than one color die?

Yes. You can roll from one to six color dice at the same time.

Are repeated colors fair?

Yes. Every die is rolled independently, so two or more dice can fairly land on the same color.
