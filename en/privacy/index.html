Privacy policy

Color Dice works without registration and does not send roll results to a server. This policy covers local game settings and data that may be processed if advertising is enabled.

Local game settings

Color Dice stores the selected theme, sound setting, roll count, and latest result in localStorage. The browser uses these values to restore the game on the same device. The site does not use them to collect a name, address, precise location, or payment information.

Advertising and cookies

No advertising code is installed as of July 18, 2026. If Google AdSense ads are enabled, third-party vendors, including Google, may use cookies to serve ads based on a user's previous visits to this site or other websites. Google's advertising cookies allow Google and its partners to choose ads based on those visits.

Google and its advertising partners may also place and read cookies or use web beacons, IP addresses, and other identifiers. These technologies support ad delivery, frequency control, measurement, and personalization according to the user's choices. Google explains this processing on its Business Data Responsibility page.

If other vendors or ad networks are enabled before advertising launches, this policy will list their names, links, and available privacy choices. You can opt out of personalized Google advertising in Google Ads Settings. To opt out of personalization from other participating vendors, visit www.aboutads.info/choices.

Advertising consent

Before personalized ads are enabled for visitors in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the site must use a Google-certified consent management platform. Its message will let visitors consent, refuse, or manage their choices. Personalized ads will not be requested without the required consent signal. Depending on the visitor's choice, Google may serve non-personalized or limited ads where supported.

Delete local data

External links and reports

Bug reports are created on GitHub and are public. GitHub processes information under its own policy. Do not include personal or confidential information in a report.
